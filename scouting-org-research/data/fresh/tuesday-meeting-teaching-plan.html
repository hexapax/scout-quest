The EDGE Method — How Scouts Teach Scouts

The BSA EDGE method is how older scouts teach younger scouts. It's the foundation of the patrol method — youth-led, hands-on learning.

E
Explain — Tell them what you're going to teach and why it matters. "Today I'm going to show you how to tie a bowline. You'll use this to rescue someone or secure a rope around a post."
D
Demonstrate — Show them how to do it, step by step, slowly enough to follow. Do it correctly the first time.
G
Guide — Have them try it while you watch and coach. Give corrections and encouragement. "Good, but pull the working end tighter."
E
Enable — Let them do it on their own. Step back and let them practice until they've got it. Then test them — can they do it without help?

Sign-Off Protocol

When a learner completes a requirement at a station, the teaching scout initials the learner's handbook and writes the date. After the meeting, the Scoutmaster or designated leader enters completions into Scoutbook. Requirements should only be signed off when the scout can demonstrate the skill without assistance (the "Enable" step of EDGE).
