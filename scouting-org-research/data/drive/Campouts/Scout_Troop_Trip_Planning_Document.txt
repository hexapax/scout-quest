1. Weekend 1: Feb 13 – 15, 2026 (Presidents' Day)
School Holiday Monday. Ideal for longer distances or local festivals.
Option A: The "Big Trip" – Okefenokee Swamp
* The Draw: Winter is the only time to comfortably camp here (no bugs, safe gators). A unique ecosystem experience.
* The Activity: Paddling canoes/motorboats into the refuge. International Dark Sky Park (Astronomy MB).
* The Camping: Stephen C. Foster State Park (Fargo, GA). Excellent comfort stations and cottages.
* Drive Time: ~4.5 hours.
Option B: Chattanooga History (VIP Connection)
* The Draw: Exclusive history experience with a subject matter expert.
* The Activity: Tour the Medal of Honor Heritage Center or Chickamauga Battlefield.
   * Contact: Brad Quinlin (21stohio@charter.net). We can coordinate a "Soldier's Story" tour with him.
* The Camping: Chattanooga Audubon Society (Audubon Acres). Dedicated Scout sites, fire rings, semi-primitive but near town.
* Drive Time: ~2 hours.
Option C: Stone Mountain Lunar New Year
* The Draw: High-entertainment value close to home.
* The Activity: Lunar New Year Festival. Drone shows, parades, and cultural performances.
* The Camping: Stone Mountain Park Campground. They have dedicated scout sites or Yurts.
* Drive Time: < 45 mins.
Option D: Winter Hiking & Crafts (Helen)
* The Draw: Mountain feel with indoor activities if it rains/snows.
* The Activity: Fireside Arts & Craft Show at Unicoi (indoors) + Hike Anna Ruby Falls.
* The Camping: Unicoi State Park. "Squirrel's Nest" camping (covered platforms) is fun for Scouts.
* Drive Time: ~1.5 hours.

2. Weekend 2: March 20 – 22, 2026
Option A: Atlanta Science Festival (STEM)
* The Draw: The region's largest hands-on science event.
* The Activity: Exploration Expo at Piedmont Park. Robotics, biology, and chemistry booths.
* The Camping: Bert Adams Scout Camp (Covington) or Sweetwater Creek (Lithia Springs).
* Drive Time: ~45 mins to camp.
Option B: Chattanooga Charge (Green Tech)
* The Draw: Focus on the future of transport (Automotive Maintenance / Engineering MB).
* The Activity: Chattanooga Charge 2026. EV and tech expo at the Tennessee Riverpark.
* The Camping: Harrison Bay State Park. Large group camp, marina access.
* Drive Time: ~2.25 hours.
Option C: Macon Cherry Blossom Festival
* The Draw: "The Pinkest Party on Earth." Classic festival fun.
* The Activity: Opening weekend festival with midway rides, food trucks, and exhibits.
* The Camping: Indian Springs State Park or High Falls State Park. Both have excellent hiking/waterfalls nearby.
* Drive Time: ~1.5 hours.

3. Weekend 3: March 27 – 29, 2026
Option A: The "Cast & Blast" (Fishing Focus) we likey
* The Draw: The best fishing access for a large group in the state.
* The Activity: Fishing at Marben PFA (Charlie Elliott Wildlife Center). 22 managed ponds for bass/bream.
   * Bonus: Jon Wood Astronomy Field is on-site for star parties.
* The Camping: Charlie Elliott Campground. Cleaner and quieter than most state parks. Central shower house.
* Drive Time: ~1 hour.
Option B: Military Heritage (Columbus)
* The Draw: Immersive military history and primitive "trench" camping.
* The Activity: National Infantry Museum. WWI trench simulations, "Last 100 Yards" exhibit.
* The Camping: Camp NIM (National Infantry Museum). Primitive camping on museum grounds. (Or FDR State Park for showers).
* Drive Time: ~1.75 hours.
Option C: Aviation History (Warner Robins)
* The Draw: STEM and Flight.
* The Activity: Museum of Aviation. 2nd largest USAF museum. F-15s, SR-71s, and Merit Badge classes (Aviation, Weather).
* The Camping: High Falls State Park.
* Drive Time: ~1.5 hours.
Option D: Atlanta Heritage Hike
* The Draw: Citizenship and urban history.
* The Activity: AAC Civil Rights Heritage Hike. 5-mile urban hike visiting historic sites.
* The Camping: Red Top Mountain State Park.
* Drive Time: < 1 hour.

4. Weekend 4: April 24 – 26, 2026
Option A: Talladega Superspeedway (Primary Recommendation) we likey
* The Draw: Unique "Scout Days" program. High excitement.
* The Activity: Saturday Xfinity Race, camping in Scout Zone, Sunday morning "Track Walk" on the banking.
* The Camping: Talladega Scout Campground. Note: Portable toilets/shower trailers only. No shade.
* Drive Time: ~1.75 hours (Central Time).
Option B: Georgia Renaissance Festival
* The Draw: Fun, turkey legs, and blacksmithing demos.
* The Activity: Renaissance Festival in Fairburn.
* The Camping: McIntosh Reserve Park. Massive county park on the river. Allows ground fires. Very secluded.
* Drive Time: ~1 hour.
Option C: Chattanooga Seafood Bash
* The Draw: Food and riverfront atmosphere.
* The Activity: Seafood Bash at Ross's Landing.
* The Camping: Chester Frost Park. Excellent facilities on a peninsula, but fills up fast with fishermen.
* Drive Time: ~2 hours.

5. Appendix: Planning Factors
Weather & Seasonality
* February: Highs in 50s, Lows in 30s. Risk of freezing rain. Okefenokee is warmer (60s/70s).
* March: "Mud Season." Expect rain. Pollen starts late March.
* April: Highs in 70s. Storm risk. Talladega has high sun exposure (bring shade).
