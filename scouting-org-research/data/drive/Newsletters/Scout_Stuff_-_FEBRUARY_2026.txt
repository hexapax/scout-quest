NEW in 2026 - BOARD OF REVIEW PROCESS
Announcing our new Board of Review Process in 2026:
Board of Review Committee Chair: Eric Buffenbarger
Board of Review Committee Members: Chaffee Heilman, Blake Shirley, Abbey Flaum
Starting in 2026, we will require all Scouts hoping to earn rank advancement at any given Court of Honor to first undergo a Board of Review (BOR). Our Board of Review committee, chaired by Eric Buffenbarger, will be offering these Board of Review sessions after the 2nd Troop Meeting of each month. So for example, our Troop Meetings are listed on the Scoutbook calendar, and now we have the BORs listed as a separate event right afterwards. Scouts must sign up for these BORs in advance; otherwise, if no Scouts need it, the BOR session will be cancelled.
These BORs are anticipated to take around 10-15 minutes per Scout.
There is not much Scouts need to do to prepare for the BOR. It is merely a casual, friendly conservation between the Scout and the BOR committee members. They are going to ask the Scout questions about how they felt about the rank requirements they earned since the last rank, and the merit badges they earned since their last rank.


Before a Scout walks into that BOR, the only two things they need to know are:
(1) which rank am I trying to earn right now? (they should glance over the requirements to remind themselves what they were). I.e. Scout/Second Class/Star/Life. If the Scout doesn’t know that, it’s a real problem and they will be excused from the BOR to try again another time.
(2) which merit badges did I complete since my last rank? Again, the Scout should know this. If they completed 8 merit badges and forget to mention a couple, not a big deal. If they sit down with a blank stare and no understanding of what they’ve worked on, that’s a problem.
When the time comes, the BOR committee is just going to chat with the Scout about the merit badges - what did you like about the MB? or not like? how long did it take you? what was the hardest MB requirement and why? Would you recommend this MB to other Scouts? Which Life rank requirement was the hardest, best, most fun, etc?
Why are we now requiring BORs? The reason our Troop will now require Scouts to sit for these BORs before each rank is because it is what is best for the Scout. These BORs by our committee will mimic what Scouts will experience when they sit for their Eagle Board of Review one day. I want them to have experience with such BORs before their Eagle one. It will build their confidence leading up to their ultimate Eagle BOR. Please contact Eric Buffenbarger or Nicole Allen with any questions.

SWIM TEST
Every year at summer camp, all Scouts are required to take the BSA swim test upon arrival. (We typically do this Sunday afternoon a few hours after we get checked in).Since Pace students swim at school, most of our boys easily earn their SWIMMER designation. Scouts must be designated as a “Swimmer” before they are allowed to swim in the lake for fun (with life jackets of course).  Below are some details on what this test involves:


BSA SWIMMER’S TEST: Jump feet first into water over the head in depth, level off, and begin swimming. Swim 75 yards in a strong manner using one or more of the following strokes: side stroke, breaststroke, trudgen, or crawl; then swim 25 yards using an easy resting back stroke. The 100 yards must be swum continuously and include at least one sharp turn. After completing the swim, rest by floating. 
* or - 
BEGINNER’S TEST: Jump feet first into water over the head in depth, level off, swim 25 feet on the surface, stop, turn sharply, resume swimming as before, and return to starting place. 


Anyone who has not completed the beginner or swimmer tests is classified as a nonswimmer.
