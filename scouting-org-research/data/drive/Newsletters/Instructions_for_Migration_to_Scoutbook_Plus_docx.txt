Instructions for transitioning to Scoutbook Plus


ACTION REQUESTED:
We are transitioning to Scoutbook, and need your help. We are moving over ALL our advancement and ALL our calendar events (with RSVPs!) to Scoutbook, with the hope that we can discontinue our subscription to both Troopmaser & Teamsnap by the end of the year, saving the troop money!
Here are the steps we need each parents to take:




Step 1
	

	Create account(s) on My.Scouting.Org
	

	𐄂
	In your web browser, type my.scouting.org - make sure you have/know your own log-in credentials for yourself, as the parent. Once you’ve made note of those, log out and then Create a New Account for your Scout(s) by following the prompts. Additional instructions to do this are attached, but you probably won’t need them.
	

	𐄂
	If you need your Scouts BSA ID#, contact Nicole Texnicking@gmail.com. 
	

	𐄂
	After you’ve finished creating an account for your Scout(s), log into each account separately and follow instructions for Step 2
	

	Step 2
	

	Enter contact information into Profile page
	

	𐄂
	Go to my.scouting.org and log in
	

	𐄂
	Hover your browser pointer over the blue box on the far right that says “Scouting America Web Links”, then select “Scoutbook Plus”
	

	𐄂
	You may be required to log in again
	

	𐄂
	On the menu, click on Profile
	

	

	On the top right of Profile page, click on the button that says “Personal Info”
	

	𐄂
	Scroll down until you see the “contact information” section
	

	𐄂
	Confirm or add an email address
	

	𐄂
	Confirm or add a phone number
	

	𐄂
	Add a mobile phone number & carrier and circle circle on left to set as primary number
	

	𐄂
	Recommended: If you wish to receive text alerts from troop leaders (typcially last minute instructions or updates for campouts), check the box next to “I would like to receive text messages from Scouting America (i.e. communication about my unit's outings such as arrival and departure times, event updates, etc.)
	

	𐄂
	Click on blue “save changes” button on bottom right
	

	𐄂
	Repeat step 2 for all individual accounts (mother, father, scout 1, scout 2, etc), then move onto Step 3
	

	Step 3
	

	Confirm Merit Badges, Rank Requirements and Positions of Responsibility
	

	𐄂
	It’s time to review your scout(s) advancement in Scoutbook. At this point, all data related to merit badges, rank advancement and positions of responsibility have been transferred into Scoutbook Plus. If something is missing or incorrect, please contact our Advancement Chair, Nicole Allen: texnicking@gmail.com
	

	𐄂
	After you log into my.scouting.org, go to Scoutbook Plus (directions above in Step 2) and go to Profile. 
	

	𐄂
	Look over everything on that Profile page. 
	

	𐄂
	In the section called “Scouts BSA Ranks” (middle top of page) - everything should be up-to-date (click on “View More” blue button to see more details.)
	

	𐄂
	In the section called “Merit Badges” (middle, lower of page) - all pending and approved merit badges will be listed. “Pending” means in-progress. “Approved” means completed and credit applied. “Awarded” means the Scout has received the physical badge for their uniform.
	

	𐄂
	In the section called “Youth Positions” (left lower area of page), all current and past Positions of Responsibility (POR) should be listed. If something is missing or incorrect, please contact our Scoutmaster Jeremy Bramwell: jebramwell@gmail.com.
	

	Step 4
	

	Activity Logs
	

	𐄂
	When you’re on the Scout’s “Profile” page, you’ll see a section called “Activity Logs” (right middle of page). This is where the Scout can enter his camping nights and/or his service hours for approval. Below are the instructions for each
	

	

	Camping
	

	𐄂
	In Activity Log section, click on the word “Camping”
	

	𐄂
	Click blue circle that says Create or Join+
	

	𐄂
	Find the date of the campout.  The date should be highlighted with a light blue box. Under the calendar the campout should be listed and click Join
	

	𐄂
	Next you will see the details of the campout.  At the bottom you need to first toggle to the “Individual” tab (not the group tab), then add the number of days and nights (typical campouts are 2 days/1 night), and then click Join Activity.
	

	𐄂
	When you return to your activity log, the camping activity should show “marked as completed”. This item will then be sent to the Advancement Chair for approval. Once approved by the Advancement Chair, the item should show as “approved/recorded”. Give it about a week and if it hasn’t been approved, feel free to email Nicole Allen.
	

	𐄂
	Once you return to the Profile Page, these camping nights should show up under the camping section of the Activity Log. These camping nights will be used to (1) approve Camping Merit Badge requirement 9a, and (2) determine eligibility for candidacy for the Order of the Arrow honor society.
	

	

	Optional: Any Scout who has not yet earned their Camping MB is required to add their camping nights to Scoutbook Plus. Any Scout who has already completed their Camping MB can opt out of entering their camping nights into Scoutbook; however, we recommend you do it anyway as a way to track all your requirements for Eagle in one place. You will find it helpful when it comes time to go in front of the Eagle Board of Review!
	

	

	Hiking
	

	𐄂
	Our troop hasn’t traditionally done a ton of hiking, but feel free to log hiking details here if the Scout plans to work on their Hiking Merit Badge
	

	

	Service Hours
	

	𐄂
	In Activity Log section, click on the words “Service Hours”
	

	𐄂
	Click blue circle that says Create or Join+
	

	𐄂
	Find the date of the service project.  The date should be highlighted with a light blue box. Under the calendar the service project should be listed and click Join
	

	𐄂
	If the service project is not listed, please email date/time/project details to Nicole Allen so she can add it to the calendar first, then you can Join.
	

	𐄂
	Next you will see the details of the service project.  At the bottom you need to first toggle to the “Individual” tab (not the group tab), then add the number of service hours, and then click Join Activity.
	

	𐄂
	When you return to your activity log, the service hours activity should show “marked as completed”. This item will then be sent to the Advancement Chair for approval. Once approved by the Advancement Chair, the item should show as “approved/recorded”. Give it about a week and if it hasn’t been approved, feel free to email Nicole Allen.
	

	𐄂
	Once you return to the Profile Page, these service hours should show up under the Service Hours section of the Activity Log. These service hours will be used to (1) approve rank requirements and (2) approve MB requirements such as the Camping MB reqt 9c, if the service hours were conservation-related and earned at a campout.
	

	Step 5
	

	Learn how to send emails and text messages within the Troop
	

	𐄂
	Go to my.scouting.org and log in (Alternatively, if you are already in Scoutbook Plus, on the left menu you can click on the words “Scoutbook Legacy”)
	

	𐄂
	Hover your browser pointer over the blue box on the far right that says “Scouting America Web Links”, then select “Scoutbook (Legacy)”. It will likely require you to log in again.
	

	𐄂
	Assuming you only have a Scout in the Troop (not the Cub Scout Pack 4), it should default to showing you the dashboard page for Troop 2024. (If you have boys in both, then select Troop 2024 first)
	

	𐄂
	Once on the Dashboard page, scroll down until you see an option for “Send Message 📧” and click there
	

	𐄂
	For some, you’ll need to toggle between Pack 4 and Troop 2024 at the top so you can see the contacts you want.
	

	𐄂
	Select the contacts to which you wish to send an email OR a text message.
You can choose any combination of Leaders and Parents and Youth Members. 
Youth Members will ONLY be listed if they have created their OWN account and opted into communications (see Steps 1 & 2 above).
	

	𐄂
	Scroll down….
	

	𐄂
	In the middle of the page, you’ll need to click on one of the two buttons shown: Send Email or Send Text
	

	𐄂
	For emails, fill in the fields, attach files and calendar events as desired and then click on “Send Message”
	

	𐄂
	For texts, enter text up to 160 characters and then click on “Send Text”
	

	𐄂
	If someone has an email address listed on their profile page but not a mobile number, they will receive the text as an email.
	

	𐄂
	If someone has a mobile phone number listed and has opted in to receive text messages (see Step 2 above), they will receive a text to their phone.
	

	𐄂
	Text messages will be sent from a do.not.reply messaging service. So it will not be a typical group text chain full of comments to which you can respond. It will be individual texts sent from Scoutbook (Legacy) - much like a weather alert would appear.
