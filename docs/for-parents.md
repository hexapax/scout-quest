# Scout Quest: A Guide for Parents & Guardians

## What is Scout Quest?

Scout Quest is an AI assistant for Troop 2024 that connects to your scout's real advancement data in Scoutbook. You can ask it questions in plain English and get answers based on actual records — not guesses.

## What Can You Do With It?

**Check your scout's progress:**
- "How is my scout doing on advancement?"
- "What rank is my scout working on?"
- "What merit badges has my scout earned?"
- "What requirements does my scout still need for First Class?"

**Stay informed about troop activities:**
- "What events are coming up this month?"
- "Did my scout RSVP for the campout?"
- "When is the next troop meeting?"

**Understand BSA procedures:**
- "How does the Eagle Scout process work?"
- "What are the requirements for Star rank?"
- "Do partial merit badge completions expire?" (They don't.)
- "What is a Board of Review?"

**Support your scout at home:**
- "What can my scout work on at home this week?"
- "How can I help my scout prepare for the camping trip?"
- "What's the buddy system and why does it matter?"

## How to Use It

1. Go to **scout-quest.hexapax.com**
2. Log in with the Google account associated with your Scoutbook profile
3. The system recognizes you as a parent from the troop roster and shows your scout's information
4. Just type your question — no special commands needed

## Safety and Privacy

**Youth Protection is built in.** Scout Quest follows BSA Youth Protection Training (YPT) guidelines:

- If the system helps draft any communication, parents are always CC'd
- You can only see your own scout's data — not other families' information
- The AI does not have private conversations with scouts that parents can't see
- All data comes from Scoutbook, the official BSA record system

**Your data stays private.** Scout Quest runs on private infrastructure managed by our troop's Scoutmaster. Conversations are not stored long-term and are not shared with other families or used to train AI models.

## What It Won't Do

- It won't do your scout's work for them. It coaches and guides — the scout does the learning.
- It won't sign off on requirements. Only the Scoutmaster or a merit badge counselor can do that.
- It won't replace direct communication with leaders. For urgent matters, contact your Scoutmaster directly.
