# Scout Quest: A Guide for Troop Leaders

## What You Get

Scout Quest gives you an AI assistant that knows your troop's advancement data — every scout's rank progress, merit badge status, requirement completion, and upcoming events — and can answer questions, generate reports, and plan advancement activities.

The data comes directly from Scoutbook, synced periodically. The AI has a curated BSA knowledge base covering rank requirements, the Guide to Advancement, the Guide to Safe Scouting, and Eagle-required merit badge details.

## Troop-Wide Tools

### Advancement Dashboard

Ask: **"Show me troop progress"** or **"How is the troop doing on advancement?"**

Returns a dashboard showing every scout's current rank, next rank in progress (with % complete), merit badges earned, and who's close to ranking up. Identifies bottlenecks — scouts who are stalled or close to a milestone.

### Who Needs What

Ask: **"Who still needs Tenderfoot?"** or **"Who still needs First Class 7b?"**

Returns every scout who hasn't completed that rank or specific requirement, sorted by how close they are. Useful for identifying groups to work with at meetings.

### Who Can Teach

Ask: **"Who can teach first aid?"** or **"Who can help with navigation?"**

Returns scouts who have completed requirements in that skill area, with EDGE-trained scouts highlighted. These are your best peer instructors — scouts who have completed the Teaching EDGE requirement (Life Req 6) are proven teachers.

### Teach/Learn Pairings

Ask: **"Pair up scouts for first aid practice"** or **"Generate pairings for cooking skills"**

Matches older scouts who've completed requirements with younger scouts who need them. Prioritizes EDGE-trained scouts as mentors. This is the collaborative advancement engine — an older scout gets leadership practice while a younger scout gets skill instruction.

## Planning Advancement Events

The session planner is designed for events like a Sunday advancement day. Give it who's coming, how much time you have, and what leaders are available.

Ask: **"Plan a 2-hour advancement session for Will, Henry, Jack, and Claude with Mr. Chaffe and Mr. Bramwell helping"**

The system generates:

- **Skill stations** grouped by area (first aid, navigation, knots, cooking, etc.), prioritized by how many attending scouts need work in each area
- **Peer instructor assignments** — older scouts who can lead each station
- **Leader assignments** — which adult should cover which station
- **Equipment lists** — what to bring for each station
- **Multi-person requirements** — requirements that physically need partners (line rescue, stretcher transport, buddy system skills), scheduled so partners are available at the same time
- **Per-scout checklists** — what each scout can potentially complete during the session

### How BSA's Collaborative Requirements Work

The BSA program deliberately builds a teaching ladder:

1. **Tenderfoot**: Learn the EDGE method, teach someone the square knot
2. **Star/Life**: Teach a full skill set to a younger scout using Teaching EDGE
3. **Eagle**: Lead an entire service project with volunteers

Many requirements also explicitly require working with another person — First Class 6e (line rescue) needs three people, First Class 7b (transport) needs a partner, and patrol meals need a team.

Scout Quest surfaces these collaborative requirements and matches scouts who need them with scouts who can help. When an older scout teaches a younger one, both advance — the younger scout learns the skill, and the older scout practices the leadership and instruction that Star, Life, and Eagle demand.

## Policy and Procedure Support

Scout Quest has comprehensive BSA policy knowledge. Leaders can ask about:

- Board of Review rules and procedures
- Advancement policies (partial completions, time-sensitive requirements, appeals)
- Youth Protection requirements
- Guide to Safe Scouting rules for specific activities
- Eagle project requirements and process
- Leadership position requirements for Star, Life, and Eagle

The AI provides specific citations from the Guide to Advancement when helpful, and explains the reasoning behind counter-intuitive policies.

## How to Use It

1. Go to **scout-quest.hexapax.com** and log in with your Google account
2. The system recognizes your role from the Scoutbook roster
3. Ask questions in plain English — no special syntax needed
4. For session planning, include scout names, time available, and leaders present

## Data and Privacy

- All data comes from Scoutbook and stays on our private infrastructure
- Leaders see troop-wide data; parents see only their scout
- The system follows YPT guidelines in all communications
- Scoutbook remains the official record — Scout Quest reads from it but doesn't replace it
