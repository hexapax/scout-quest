The Scouting America (formerly BSA) merit badge blue card process begins with the Scout obtaining a signed **Application for Merit Badge** (No. 34124 or 34130) from their unit leader, who approves the merit badge choice, provides registered counselor contacts, and signs the front before giving the full card to the Scout.[3][4][8]

### How to Get a Blue Card
- Scouts request the card from their unit leader (e.g., Scoutmaster), discussing their interest and approach; the leader signs it first and provides approved merit badge counselor names and contact info.[1][3][4]
- Physical blue cards are available in packs of 25 or 100 from Scout Shops or printable via Scoutbook templates; fillable PDF versions exist for electronic use, where the unit leader signs digitally after Scout request.[3][9]

### Counselor Assignment
- After unit leader signature, the Scout (with a buddy, per Youth Protection guidelines) contacts a registered counselor from the provided list, schedules sessions, and works on requirements.[3][4]
- The unit leader must approve counselors in advance; Scouts cannot self-select without this step.[1][3]

### Working on Requirements and Partial Completions
- The counselor initials completed requirements on the card as partial progress is made; no full signature until all requirements are met.[4]
- If incomplete, the counselor initials fulfilled parts but does not sign off completion; the Scout retains the card and can resume with the same or new counselor later.[1][4]

### Completion and Signatures
- Upon full completion, the counselor signs twice: on the reverse of the Application portion (left side) and the Applicant’s Record (middle).[1][3][4]
- The Scout returns the card to the unit leader, who verifies completion, signs the Applicant’s Record, retains the Application portion, and gives the Applicant’s Record to the Scout as a permanent record.[1][3][4][5]
- The troop's Advancement Chair verifies the card, enters it into Internet Advancement (or Scoutbook), files it (ideally until the Scout is 18 + 3 months), and uses reports for badge pickup at the Scout Shop.[2]

### Counselor Changes
- Partial work carries over; Scouts can switch counselors, who review prior initials and continue from there—no need to restart unless requirements changed.[4] (Guide to Advancement implies continuity via signed/unsigned sections.)

### Troop Transfers
- Search results do not explicitly detail transfers, but the Applicant's Record serves as portable proof; the new unit leader reviews it (with counselor signatures/initials) to confirm and report completion.[3][4] Units file cards and report via Internet Advancement for cross-verification.[2]

### Digital Alternatives in Scoutbook
- Scoutbook is an authorized digital method alongside paper blue cards, tracking progress electronically for Scouts, counselors, and leaders.[5][7]
- Generate printable blue card templates from Scoutbook; enter completions directly for reporting—no paper needed if fully digital, though some units print for records or remote counselors (e.g., white paper equivalents).[1][3][7]
- Both methods remain valid; units may hybridize for hard copies.[1][7]
