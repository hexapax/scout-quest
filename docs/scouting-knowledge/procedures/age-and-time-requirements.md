**Scouts BSA ranks (Scout through Eagle) have no minimum age requirements except that youth must be at least 11 years old (or have completed fifth grade or earned the Arrow of Light Award) to join Scouts BSA.** All ranks, merit badges, and Eagle Palms must generally be earned before the youth's 18th birthday, though Eagle Scout boards of review can occur up to 24 months after (with special approval beyond that) and limited extensions are available for unavoidable circumstances.[1][2]

### Time-in-Rank Requirements
Time-in-rank applies only to **Star, Life, and Eagle** (no time requirements for Scout, Tenderfoot, Second Class, or First Class):
- **Star**: Minimum 4 months as First Class.[6]
- **Life**: Minimum 6 months as Star.[6]
- **Eagle**: Minimum 6 months as Life.[6]

These periods ensure progressive leadership and service; they run concurrently with other requirements like merit badges or positions of responsibility.

### Full Rank Age and Eligibility Details
| Rank          | Minimum Age to Earn | Time-in-Rank Requirement | Key Notes |
|---------------|---------------------|---------------------------|-----------|
| **Scout**    | None (join at 11)  | None                     | Entry rank; basic Scout Oath, Law, and joining requirements.[2] |
| **Tenderfoot**| None               | None                     | Camping, fitness, citizenship skills.[2] |
| **Second Class** | None            | None                     | Advanced outdoor and safety skills.[2] |
| **First Class** | None             | None                     | Proficiency in leadership, first aid, navigation.[2] |
| **Star**     | None               | 4 months minimum as First Class | 6 merit badges; leadership position; service hours.[6] |
| **Life**     | None               | 6 months minimum as Star | 5 more merit badges; leadership; service project.[6] |
| **Eagle**    | None               | 6 months minimum as Life | 12 total Eagle-required merit badges (10 if Quartermaster); Eagle project; board of review (may be after 18th birthday).[1][2] |

Registered Scouts (including Lone Scouts), qualified Venturers, and Sea Scouts (those who earned First Class in Scouts BSA) may advance up to age 18.[1][2] Exceptions for disabilities allow continued work post-18 with council approval and possible alternative merit badges.[1] Limited time extensions for Eagle are rare and only for circumstances beyond the Scout's control.[2][3]
