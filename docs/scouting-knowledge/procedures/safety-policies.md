### Safe Swim Defense
**Safe Swim Defense** is Scouting America's policy for all swimming activities, requiring trained adult supervision and adherence to eight key points.[1][5] Leaders must complete training within the previous two years and ensure compliance at pools, beaches, or temporary waterfronts (excluding boating covered by Safety Afloat).[1][5]

Key rules for leaders:
- **Qualified Supervision**: Supervised by a mature adult 21+ trained in the eight points; recommend unit lifeguard or aquatics supervision training.[3][5]
- **Personal Health Review**: Require completed Annual Health and Medical Record.[2][4]
- **Safe Area**: Inspect area beforehand for hazards; define buddy-tagged zones by ability (nonswimmers ≤3.5 ft deep, beginners to head-deep, swimmers ≤12 ft in clear water); control access and mark boundaries.[2][3][5]
- **Response Personnel**: Station trained lifeguards/rescue team ready for emergencies.[2][4][5]
- **Lookout**: Dedicated person monitors conduct, rules violations, weather, and environment.[2][4]
- **Ability Groups**: Classify all as swimmers (100-yard swim test), beginners (50-yard swim), or nonswimmers via standardized tests.[2][3][5]
- **Buddy System**: Pair swimmers, beginners, and nonswimmers; buddies check in regularly.[3][5] (Inferred from standard policy in sources.)
- **Skill Proficiency**: Match activities to tested ability; no diving >40 inches or in <7 ft clear water; restrict in deep/turbid/flowing water unless using life jackets.[3][5]

### Safety Afloat
**Safety Afloat** governs open-water boating activities like rowing, canoeing, sailing, motorboating, and waterskiing (not swimming).[3] Leaders must be trained (details inferred as parallel to Safe Swim Defense).[7]

Key rules for leaders (nine defenses from sources):
- All on open water must wear properly fitted U.S. Coast Guard-approved PFDs.[3]
- Ensure qualified supervision by trained adults 21+.[3] (Parallel to Safe Swim.)
- Conduct pre-trip health reviews and inspections.[3] (Inferred structure.)
- Define safe areas, station response personnel, use lookouts, group by ability, buddy system, and proficiency.[3]
