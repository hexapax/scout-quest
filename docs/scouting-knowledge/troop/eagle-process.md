## Practice Eagle Board of Review Questions

The troop maintains a list of practice questions for Eagle candidates. These reflect actual questions recalled from previous Eagle Boards and internet research:

### Scouting Reflection
1. Scouting views becoming an Eagle Scout as just the beginning of your journey. How do you plan to stay involved in Scouting going forward? (Must have an answer for this)
2. What is the difference between GETTING Eagle and BEING Eagle?
3. The first eight words of the Scout Oath are "On my honor I will do my best." What does honor mean to you?

### Camping & Merit Badges
4. What was the worst campout you went on? What happened and how did you overcome it?
5. What was the best campout you went on?
6. When was the last camping trip you took with your troop?
7. What was your favorite merit badge?
8. Which badge was the least helpful or needs to be retired?

### Scout Law & Values
9. What point of the Scout Law do you think is the hardest for youth today to follow? Why?
10. What point of the Scout Law means the most to you?
11. If you had to add a point to the Scout Law, what would it be?
12. There are 21 merit badges required for Eagle. If you had to add one more, which one and why?

### Personal Growth
13. If you could start over, would you join Scouting?
14. What would you tell a Scout who is just starting?
15. What would you change about your experience with Scouting?
16. How do you balance accomplishing Eagle with the perception that Scouting isn't cool?

### Eagle Project
17. How did you pick this project?
18. What was the most satisfying part?
19. What was the most difficult part?
20. If you had to do it over, what would you do differently?
21. How did you teach others to do the tasks required?

### Leadership
18. What was the most challenging part of leading other Scouts?
19. Who is harder to lead, children or adults?

### Future Plans
20. Outside of Scouting, what do you like to do?
21. Plans for remainder of high school?
22. College plans?
23. Career interests?
24. How has Scouting prepared you for the future?
25. What question were you worried we would ask?

### Tips for Success (from troop guidance)

- Be familiar with every word in your application
- Practice answers out loud
- Remember who wrote your letters of recommendation and why you chose them
- Be confident - you earned the right to be there
- Try to enjoy the process
- **Do NOT mention** that meetings/badge classes were on Zoom during COVID (troop was an outlier in not meeting in person; will get uncomfortable questions)
- **High Adventure question:** If asked, say "I've heard about High Adventure, but our troop hasn't participated yet. However, I'll certainly consider it for next summer." (Troop has historically been too new and small for High Adventure)
- **Young candidates:** If under Junior year, expect questions about age. Fine to mention COVID lockdown gave extra time for advancement work
