# Troop 2024 Campouts and Events

## Campout Philosophy

- Troop aims for one campout per month during the school year (typically September through April)
- No campouts in May (Scouts too busy), August (too hot start), or December (service project instead)
- July typically too hot for camping
- Two-deep leadership required: must have registered, trained adult with IOLS and Hazardous Weather
- Scouts required to log at least 20 camping nights for Eagle rank
- Parents welcome to camp with the troop

## Summer Camp - Woodruff Scout Camp

The troop attends Woodruff Scout Camp annually (typically first week of June).

### Key Details

- **Cost (2026):** $525 per Scout
- **Duration:** Sunday through Saturday (~6 nights)
- **Payment Due:** March 1 (to Treasurer via check to Pace Academy or Venmo)
- **Location:** Woodruff Scout Reservation (northeast Georgia mountains)
- **High Adventure:** One activity included ($50 each additional)
  - Horseback riding
  - Whitewater rafting on the Nantahala (fulfills Camping MB 9b)
- **Cell Phone Policy:** No cell phone use at camp; adult chaperones allow calls home if needed. AT&T gets service; Verizon only at Dining Hall.
- **Swim Test Required:** BSA Swimmer's Test upon arrival (100 yards continuous swim)

### Chaperone System

Parents volunteer for specific nights. Need 2+ adults present at all times. Woodruff adult leader fee: ~$150/person (paid from troop budget, historically $400-$495 total).

## Conservation Weekend

- Annual event at Woodruff Scout Camp, typically November
- Scouts can attend as provisional (sign up individually)
- Cost: ~$100/Scout
- Can camp 1 or 2 nights (no discount for 1 night)
- Merit badge classes offered (3 per Scout)
- Conservation service hours earned
- 2023 registrations included classes like Geology, Oceanography, Cit World, Fishing, Reptile & Amphibian Study, Fly Fishing, and conservation projects
