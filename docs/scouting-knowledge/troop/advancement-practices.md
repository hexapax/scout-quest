## Board of Review Process (New in 2026)

### Overview

Starting in 2026, all Scouts must complete a Board of Review before earning rank advancement at any Court of Honor. This is a new process implemented to prepare Scouts for their eventual Eagle Board of Review.

### BOR Committee

- **Chair:** Eric Buffenbarger
- **Members:** Chaffee Heilman, Blake Shirley, Abbey Flaum

### Schedule & Logistics

- Offered after the 2nd troop meeting of each month (7:30-8:00 PM)
- Each BOR takes approximately 10-15 minutes
- Scouts must RSVP on Scoutbook calendar; if no Scouts sign up by the day before, the BOR is cancelled
- Aim for 3 adults on the board
- Spring 2026 BOR dates: January 27, February 24, March 31

### What Scouts Need to Know

Before walking into a BOR, Scouts must know:
1. Which rank they are currently working toward
2. Which merit badges they have completed since their last rank

### BOR Content

The BOR is a casual, friendly conversation. Committee members ask about:
- What did you like/not like about the merit badges you completed?
- How long did each take?
- What was the hardest requirement and why?
- Would you recommend the MB to other Scouts?
- Which rank requirement was hardest, best, most fun?

### Deadline Policy

All BORs and merit badge submissions must be completed one week before the Court of Honor. No last-minute, one-off BORs will be scheduled. Scouts must plan ahead.

## Merit Badge Opportunities

### Summer Camp (Woodruff Scout Camp)

- Primary venue for earning merit badges, especially Eagle-required ones
- Advice from leadership: schedule MBs daily (morning and afternoon); aim for at least 2 Eagle-required MBs
- Avoid aquatic MBs at Woodruff since most are available at Allatoona Aquatics Base on Saturdays over the summer
- Exception: Water Sports (water skiing/wakeboarding) only at Woodruff
- Prioritize First Aid MB if not yet completed
- Note age requirements on some MBs

### Allatoona Aquatics Base

- Saturday merit badge clinics throughout summer
- Swimming, canoeing, fishing, kayaking MBs available
- Lifesaving MB available here
- Website: https://www.scoutingatl.org/caabmb

### Conservation Weekend (Woodruff, November)

- Merit badges offered (historically): Bird Study, Energy, Environmental Science, Fish & Wildlife Management, Fishing, Forestry, Fly Fishing, Geology, Landscape Architecture, Mammal Study, Nature, Nuclear Science, Oceanography, Plant Science, Pulp and Paper, Reptile & Amphibian Study, Soil and Water Conservation, Sustainability, Weather
- Only 1 Eagle-required MB typically offered: Environmental Science (age requirement ~13)
- Cost: ~$100/Scout, ~$75/adult

## EDGE Method for Life Rank

The troop tracks EDGE (Explain, Demonstrate, Guide, Enable) teaching requirements for Life rank. Specific skill areas are assigned to senior Scouts to teach newer Scouts:
- First aid (Tenderfoot/Second Class/First Class requirements)
- Cooking and tools
- Navigation
- Lashings and camp gadgets

## Order of the Arrow Eligibility

**Requirements:**
- First Class rank or above
- 15 camping nights in the last 2 years (tracked in Scoutbook)
- Must include a long-term (5-night) camping experience

**Process:**
- Troop elections held during a regular meeting (need majority of troop in attendance)
- Spring 2026 election: March 31 meeting
