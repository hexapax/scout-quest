# Troop 2024 Policies and Customs

This file captures troop-specific policies, expectations, and operational norms extracted from all available documents. These feed into the `troop_customizations` table for the Scout Quest knowledge base.

## Meeting Policies

- **Schedule:** Every other Tuesday, 6:30-7:30 PM at Pace Academy (Knights Hall)
- **Food:** Pizza provided for Scouts at every meeting; parents update attendance on Scoutbook so correct amount is ordered
- **Uniform at meetings:** Class B (troop t-shirt) for regular meetings; Class A only required for Court of Honor
- **Parent attendance:** Parents can drop off or stay for meetings
- **Court of Honor:** Approximately twice yearly (fall and spring); Class A uniform required

## Board of Review Policy (New 2026)

- BOR required before any rank advancement at Court of Honor
- Offered after 2nd meeting of each month (7:30-8:00 PM)
- Scouts must RSVP in advance on Scoutbook; session cancelled if no signups
- No last-minute or one-off BOR sessions will be scheduled
- All rank requirement completions and MB submissions due one week before COH
- 10-15 minutes per Scout; casual conversation format

## Campout Policies

- **Frequency goal:** One campout per month (Sept-April)
- **Two-deep leadership:** Required at all campouts; must have trained adult with IOLS and Hazardous Weather
- **Adult registration:** All adults camping must be BSA-registered and YPT-certified
- **RSVP required:** Critical that families update availability on Scoutbook so patrol leaders can plan food and reserve campsites
- **Parent participation:** Parents welcome to camp with the troop
- **Pace Mountain:** Considered a Cub Scout event; BSA Scouts attend as volunteers

### Cancellation Policy

- Troop absorbs campsite cancellation fees
- Reimburse trip captain for non-shelf-stable food
- Cancel more than 1 week out: ~$250 fee
- Cancel within 1 week: 50% of site cost

## Advancement Policies

- **Tracking system:** Scoutbook (migrated from Troopmaster in late 2025)
- **Scout ownership:** Scouts must email Advancement Chairs directly and cc their parents. Parents should enable Scouts to own their journey.
- **Service hours:** Must be approved in advance by Advancement Chair or Scoutmaster, unless it's already a Scout troop/district/council event or official ICGL-sponsored event
- **No double-counting:** Service hours cannot be counted for both Pace Academy and Scouts
- **Camping nights:** Logged in Scoutbook Activity Logs; required for Scouts who haven't earned Camping MB
- **COH deadline:** All requirements and MB submissions due one week before Court of Honor; no exceptions

## Financial Policies

- **Annual troop dues:** $250 (separate from BSA registration fee)
- **Payment methods:** Check payable to Pace Academy (mailed to Treasurer) or Venmo
- **Woodruff Summer Camp:** Paid separately by participants ($525 for 2026)
- **Reimbursement:** Submit to Treasurer with attached receipt/invoice; must match amount; don't mix personal and Scout expenses; legible receipts required (scan preferred)
- **Activities:** Participants usually pay their own way for non-camping activities (rafting, climbing, etc.)

## Uniform Policy

- **Class A (Field Uniform):** Required for Court of Honor, merit badge clinics, summer camp ceremonies, and any special events
- **Class B (Activity Uniform):** Troop 2024 t-shirt, worn at regular biweekly meetings
- **Neckerchief:** Navy blue with silver lining
- **Mobilization:** Class A uniform required for emergency service response

## Communication Policies

- **Primary platform:** Scoutbook for calendar, RSVPs, advancement, and messaging
- **Newsletter:** "Scout Stuff" distributed periodically via email
- **Text alerts:** Available through Scoutbook Legacy (opt-in required; messages come from do-not-reply service)
- **Scout communication:** Scouts encouraged/expected to communicate directly with adult leaders

## Youth Leadership Policies

- **SPL election:** Elected by all Scouts in the troop
- **Patrol Leaders:** Elected by their patrol members
- **Other PORs:** Appointed by the SPL
- **POR duration:** Typically ~6 months (semester-aligned)
- **Life Scout participation:** Life Scouts must continue troop participation even after completing 6 months of POR (confirmed August 2024)
- **Scout-led model:** Troop is progressively transitioning to Scout-led format; acknowledged as an ongoing journey

## Cell Phone Policy (Summer Camp)

- No cell phone use at Woodruff Summer Camp
- Defeats the purpose of summer camp
- Parents asked to discuss policy with Scouts before camp
- Adult chaperones allow Scouts to call home if needed
- Limited cell service anyway (AT&T works; Verizon only at Dining Hall)

## Woodruff Summer Camp Policies

- **Payment deadline:** March 1
- **BSA Swim Test required:** Upon arrival (100 yards continuous)
- **MB selection:** Form distributed mid/late March
- **Guidance:** Schedule MBs daily (morning + afternoon); aim for 2+ Eagle-required; prioritize First Aid if not completed
- **High Adventure:** One included; additional $50 each; adults $50/activity
- **Parent chaperones:** Volunteer for specific nights; need 2+ adults at all times

## Emergency Mobilization Plan

The troop maintains a formal mobilization plan for emergency response:
- Activated when local emergency officials request Scout assistance
- Scoutmaster prepares scripted message and notifies SPL
- SPL activates phone tree to reach all Scouts
- Scouts report in Class A uniform with Personal Emergency Service Pack
- After-action review conducted following each drill/activation
- Plan B if phones unavailable: in-person contact via transportation

## Order of the Arrow

- **Eligibility:** First Class rank + 15 camping nights in last 2 years (including one 5-night long-term camp)
- **Election:** Held at a regular troop meeting; requires majority of troop present
- **Tracking:** Camping nights and long-term camp requirements tracked in Scoutbook

## Medical Forms

- Required for all Scouts and adults
- New forms needed annually
- Scoutmaster or designated person brings medical forms to campouts and summer camp
- Who collects/manages forms has been a recurring leadership discussion; considered having Anna Bramwell keep records

## Rechartering

- Annual process through BSA
- Scouts register individually and pay BSA fee directly
- Troop collects separate activity dues
- Recharter fees paid from troop budget (~$1,500-$2,370 historically)
