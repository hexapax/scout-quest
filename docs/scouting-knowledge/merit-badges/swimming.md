**The Swimming merit badge is one of the 13 Eagle-required merit badges (specifically, Scouts must earn one from Swimming, Hiking, or Cycling), and this status has not changed recently.** [1][2][3][5] Its requirements were updated in the 2025 Scouting America Requirements (publication 33216), so Scouts using older worksheets (e.g., in Scoutbook) may encounter the previous version—always verify with the current official pamphlet or Scouting.org for 2026.[5][8]

Below is the **full current text** of all requirements (numbered 1–10, per the 2025 update), sourced from the official Scouting America Merit Badge Hub. Practical tips follow each, based on common practices for safe, efficient completion. Note: All swimming must occur under qualified supervision in safe, tested waters, with buddy systems and proper gear.[7]

1. **Do the following:**
   - **(1a)** Tell your counselor the importance of the **Safe Swim Defense**. Explain the **buddy system** and the **safe swim area**.
   - **(1b)** Before doing ANY swimming, review and discuss the **Safe Swim Defense** with your counselor. Follow it for all swim sessions.

   **Practical tips:** Memorize the 8 points of Safe Swim Defense (e.g., qualified supervision, capabilities match). Practice buddy checks pre-swim. Tip: Use Scouting.org videos for visualization; complete this first to avoid re-dos.

2. **Do the following:**
   - **(2a)** Float face up for 5 minutes while demonstrating the **resting backstroke position**.
   - **(2b)** Rest by floating, gliding, or treading water for 2 minutes (no hands/treading allowed for first 1 minute).

   **Practical tips:** Relax arms/legs to float naturally; breathe steadily. Practice in shallow end first. Tip: If tense, exhale slowly—builds buoyancy. Use pool noodle initially for form.

3. **Do the following:**
   - **(3a)** Swim 75 yards using **breaststroke, freestyle, or sidestroke** (or a combination). Use any stroke except **elementary backstroke** or **dog paddle**.
   - **(3b)** Swim 25 yards using **elementary backstroke**.

   **Practical tips:** Break into sets (e.g., 25 yards each stroke). Focus on smooth breathing in freestyle. Tip: Film yourself for stroke critique; counselors often allow hybrid strokes if efficient. Hardest for beginners per rankings.[1]

4. **Do the following:**
   - **(4a)** Demonstrate **feetfirst** and **tuck surface dive** to a depth of 6 feet, retrieving an object.
   - **(4b)** Push off pool bottom, swim **10 yards underwater**.

   **Practical tips:** Exhale underwater for control; practice breath-holding progressively. Tip: Use marked depths; goggles help visibility. Build lung capacity with breath-hold games.

5. **Do the following:**
   - **(5a)** Swim 50 yards each of **freestyle** and **breaststroke**, **25 yards sidestroke** (left/right), and **25 yards each elementary/backstroke**.
   - **(5b)** Total: At least **150 yards** continuously using those strokes.

   **Practical tips:** Pace evenly; transition smoothly between strokes. Tip: Train in 50-yard increments; rest 20 seconds between if needed during practice. Often cited as challenging but doable with pool access.[1]

6. **Explain **swim strokes** for efficiency (freestyle, breaststroke, sidestroke, backstroke, elementary backstroke). Demonstrate **standing racing starts** and **approach/turn** for freestyle/breaststroke.**

   **Practical tips:** Watch demos on Scouting.org; practice starts from blocks. Tip: Lean forward in starts; streamline post-dive. Diagram strokes in notebook for counselor discussion.

7. **Do the following:**
   - **(7a)** Rescue a buddy 30 feet away using **feetfirst/tuck dive**, surface, swim back.
   - **(7b)** Swim 20 strokes **sidestroke** or **breaststroke** towing buddy with equipment (e.g., arm/leg).

   **Practical tips:** Use buoyant tow (e.g., kickboard); communicate with buddy. Tip: Role-play scenarios; strong swimmers pair with weaker for realism.

8. **Explain **non-swimmer/res-cue methods** (reaching, throwing, rowboat with 2 paddles/anchor). Demonstrate **reaching** and **throwing** rescues. Discuss **gear** (PFDs, ropes, ring buoys, rescue tubes, heel differential, water rescue methods).**

   **Practical tips:** Practice throws on land first (aim 50 feet). Tip: Know USCG-approved PFD types; demo in shallow water. Relates to Lifesaving prep.[1]

9. **Alone or with buddy (buddy swims too), jump into water **fully clothed** (long pants, socks, shoes, long-sleeved shirt). Remove shoes/pants; tie pants into life preserver (non-inflatable PFD).**

   **Practical tips:** Use loose clothing; practice knot (taut-line hitch). Tip: Dry run clothed; swim calmly post-removal. Builds confidence for emergencies.

10. **Visit **lifeguard HQ/service** (pool/beach). Discuss duties, training, gear, rescues. Or meet/talk with **lifeguard**.

    **Practical tips:** Schedule YWCA/pool visit; prepare 5 questions (e.g., "Worst rescue?"). Tip: Virtual option if approved; relate to Safe Swim Defense.

**Version differences:** Pre-2025 versions had minor stroke yardages/distances (e.g., Req 3 was 100 yards total) and less emphasis on clothed swims—transitioning Scouts should reprint worksheets.[5] For Eagle, complete any version started pre-2025 if signed off timely, but confirm with advancement chair. Swimming ranks low difficulty (4/10) for capable swimmers, often at summer camps.[1]
