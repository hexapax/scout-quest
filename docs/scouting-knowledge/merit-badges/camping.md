### Camping Merit Badge Status
The **Camping merit badge** is **required for the Eagle Scout rank** and has been since its inception; no recent changes to this status as of 2026[1][3][5].

### Full Current Requirements (Effective 2026)
These are the official requirements from Scouting America sources, effective January 1, 2026, with no major changes noted specifically for Camping in recent updates (though 80 merit badges were refreshed overall)[1][3][7]. Scouts beginning work before year-end may finish under prior versions or switch by December 31[3]. **Before requirements 3–8, successfully complete the Scouting America swimmer test** (details in Swimming merit badge pamphlet)[2].

1. **Do the following:**
   1. Explain to your counselor the **most likely hazards** you may encounter while participating in camping activities and what you should do to **anticipate, help prevent, mitigate, and respond** to these hazards[1].
   2. Discuss with your counselor **why it is important to be aware of weather conditions** before and during your camping activities. Tell how you can **prepare should the weather turn bad** during your campouts[1].

2. **Discuss footwear for different kinds of weather** and how the right footwear is **important for protecting your feet**[1].

3. **Explain the proper care and storage of camping equipment** (clothing, footwear, bedding)[1].

4. **List the outdoor essentials** necessary for any campout, and **explain why each item is needed**[1].

5. **Present yourself to your Scoutmaster with your pack for inspection**. Be correctly clothed and equipped for an overnight campout[1].

6. **Make a written plan for an overnight trek** and show how to get to your camping spot using a **topographical map** and **one** of the following:
   1. A compass[1].
   2. A GPS receiver[1].
   3. A smartphone with a GPS app[1].

7. **Do the following:**
   1. Make a **duty roster** showing how your patrol is organized for an actual overnight campout. List assignments for each member[1].

8. **Prepare a camp menu**. Explain how the menu would **differ from a menu for a backpacking or float trip**. Give recipes and make a food list for your patrol. **Plan two breakfasts, three lunches, and two suppers**. Discuss **how to protect your food against bad weather, animals, and contamination**[1].

9. While camping in the outdoors, **cook at least one breakfast, one lunch, and one dinner** for your patrol from the meals you have planned for requirement 8c. **At least one of those meals must be a trail meal requiring the use of a lightweight stove**[1].

10. **Explain the safety procedures for:** [Note: Full subtext cut off in sources; typically includes fire, axe, saw, etc., per standard pamphlets—verify with official pamphlet][1][6].

11. **Show experience in camping by doing the following:**
    1. **Camp a total of at least 20 nights** at designated Scouting activities or events. **One long-term camping experience of up to six consecutive nights may be applied toward this requirement**. Sleep each night under the sky or in a tent you have pitched. If the camp provides a tent that has already been pitched, you need not pitch your own tent[1][5].
    2. On any of these camping experiences, you must do **TWO** of the following, only with proper preparation and under qualified supervision:
       1. Hike up a mountain, **gaining at least 1,000 vertical feet**[1].
       2. Backpack, snowshoe, or cross-country ski for **at least four miles**[1].
       3. Take a bike trip of **at least 15 miles or at least four hours**[1].
       4. Take a **non-motorized trip on the water** of **at least four hours or 5 miles**[1].
       5. Plan and carry out an **overnight snow camping experience**[1].
       6. **Rappel down a rappel route of 30 feet or more**[1].

### Practical Tips for Each Requirement
- **Req. 1**: Review BSA's Guide to Safe Scouting for hazards like weather, wildlife, fires. Practice with scenarios; use videos for response drills. Tip: Create a hazard matrix table for counselor demo[1].
- **Req. 2**: Focus on breathable synthetics for hot/wet, wool/synthetics for cold. Tip: Bring samples to show counselor; test boots on short hikes[1].
- **Req. 3**: Air-dry gear, store in cool/dry places, use silica packs. Tip: Label gear bags; inspect post-trip for mold[1].
- **Req. 4**: Essentials: water bottle, flashlight, knife, first-aid kit, etc. Tip: Pack in a fanny pack; quiz patrol on uses[1].
- **Req. 5**: Use troop checklist; layer clothing. Tip: Practice full pack-out at home; weigh pack (<25% body weight)[1].
- **Req. 6**: Practice orienteering beforehand. Tip: Use free apps like Gaia GPS; combine with a patrol hike for fun[1].
- **Req. 7**: Rotate duties fairly (e.g., cook, cleanup). Tip: Laminate roster for camp; assign based on strengths[1].
- **Req. 8**: Balance nutrition (carbs/protein); no-cook options for bad weather. Tip: Calculate costs; shop as patrol for team-building[1].
- **Req. 9**: Use backpacking stoves like Jetboil. Tip: Practice meals at home; film for counselor if needed[1].
- **Req. 10**: Cover buddy system, tool checks. Tip: Role-play demos; reference pamphlet illustrations[1][6].
- **Req. 11a**: Track nights via Scoutbook/app; only **one** long-term (up to 6 nights) counts—**all other nights count fully** (e.g., multiple 5-night camps: one full 5-6, plus full nights from others). Clarification: Troop feedback in [1] is **incorrect**—e.g., 5-night camp counts 5 nights if it's your "one long-term," others count fully; 2-night camporees count 2 each. Discourage myths; log all with dates/proof[1].
- **Req. 11b**: Choose easy pairs like hike + water trip. Tip: Plan during camp; get leader sign-off; altitude apps for hikes[1][5].

### Version Differences Scouts Might Encounter
- **Pre-2026**: Minor wording tweaks possible (e.g., swimmer test addition in 2026)[2]; 20 nights unchanged since ~2015[1].
- **Pamphlet vs. Online**: Use www.scouting.org/meritbadges for latest; pamphlets lag annually[3][6].
- **Counselor Variations**: Some enforce strict "one long-term only, ignore extras"—**invalid per official guide**; appeal to advancement chair with logs[1][3].
