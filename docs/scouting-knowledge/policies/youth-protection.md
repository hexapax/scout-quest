Scouting America's current youth protection policies establish multiple barriers to abuse through mandatory training, leadership requirements, and strict interaction guidelines.[3][6]

## Leadership and Registration Requirements

**All registered adult leaders must complete a criminal background check and current Youth Protection training** before registration or unit charter renewal.[3][8] The **Safeguarding Youth Training** (the renamed Youth Protection Training program) must be completed every two years, and volunteers cannot be reregistered if their training is not current.[2][3]

## Two-Deep Leadership

**At least two adults over 21 must be present for all Scouting activities.**[3][9] This two-deep leadership policy applies to every unit gathering and event, ensuring no youth is ever alone with a single leader.

## One-On-One Contact Prohibition

**One-on-one contact between adult leaders and youth members is strictly prohibited both inside and outside of Scouting activities.**[3] When personal conferences are necessary, meetings must occur with the knowledge and in view of other adults or youth members.

## Communication Guidelines

**Private online communications—including texting, phone calls, chat, and instant messaging—must include another registered leader or parent.**[3] Similarly, **social media communication (Facebook, Snapchat, etc.) must include another registered leader or parent.**[3] These requirements ensure transparency and prevent isolated digital interactions.

## Mandatory Reporting Obligations

**Adult leaders and youth members have a responsibility to recognize, respond to, and report Youth Protection violations and abuse.**[3] Serious Youth Protection policy violations or behaviors that put a youth's safety at risk must be reported to the Scout executive, with online incident reporting available.[3]

## Additional Safeguards

**Campsites must be arranged to separate genders adequately** through natural barriers, physical distance, or divided camping areas.[3] The **buddy system** requires youth to stay in pairs (or groups of three when appropriate) during activities, with buddy pairs kept to the same gender where applicable.[3] Youth siblings of the same gender may share sleeping accommodations and serve as buddies with parental permission.[3]

## Prohibited Behaviors

**Physical violence, sexual activity, emotional abuse, spiritual abuse, unauthorized weapons, hazing, discrimination, harassment, bullying, cyberbullying, theft, drugs, alcohol, and pornography have no place in the Scouting program** and may result in revocation of membership.[3]
