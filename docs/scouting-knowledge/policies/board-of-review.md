# Scouting America Board of Review Procedures and Guidelines

## Board of Review Purpose and Structure

A **Board of Review determines the quality of the Scout's experience and whether the requirements for the rank have been fulfilled.**[3] It is not a retesting of requirements—the Scout has already been tested on skills and activities required for the rank.[1]

For all ranks except Eagle, the Board of Review consists of **three to six unit committee members**, with the Troop Advancement Chairperson typically serving as chairperson.[1][3] For Eagle Scout, the board consists of three to six members drawn from Scouting and the community, with at least one member of the District Advancement Committee required to serve, typically as chairperson.[1]

## Who Can and Cannot Serve

**Cannot serve:**
- Unit leaders (Scoutmaster or Assistant Scoutmasters)[1][3]
- Relatives or guardians of the Scout[1]
- The Scout's parents[3]

**Can serve:**
- Unit committee members (primary source)[1][3]
- For units with insufficient committee members, knowledgeable parents (not of the candidate) or other adults at least 21 years of age who understand Scouting's aims[3]
- Unregistered adults are permissible only as an exception, not the rule[3]

## Appropriate Questions

The board members ask open-ended questions allowing Scouts to speak about opinions, experiences, activities, and accomplishments.[1] Questions regarding home, church, school, work, athletics, and Scouting experiences are appropriate.[1]

**Scout Oath and Scout Law:** For Tenderfoot and Second Class ranks, typically just the Scout Oath and Law are asked. For higher ranks, more should be expected.[1]

**Follow-up questions:** If an answer is too brief, follow-up questions using "Why?" or "How?" prompt for more details.[1]

**Eagle-specific questions may include:**[1]
- What one thing have you gained from your Scoutmaster's conferences over the years?
- How does an Eagle Scout continue to show Scout Spirit?
- Why should this Board of Review approve your request for the Eagle Rank?

**Note on retesting:** The board should not reteach or extensively retest requirements. One or two re-tries are appropriate, especially for younger Scouts.[1]

## Duration

Regular boards of review should take approximately **15 to 30 minutes, with shorter time for lower ranks.[1] Eagle Boards of Review can be 30-45 minutes.**[3]

## Advancement Decision Process

**Board deliberation:** When all board members have asked their questions, the Scout is asked to leave the room.[1] The board members then decide if the Scout is ready for the next rank; **the board's decision must be unanimous.**[1][3]

**Communication of decision:** The Scout is called back into the room and the chairperson informs them of the board's decision.[1]

## If a Scout Is Not Approved

If a board does not approve advancement, the candidate must be informed and told what must be done to succeed at the next Board of Review.[3] The board **must detail the deficiencies so the Scout can correct them**, and the Scout must be told specifically what actions are needed.[1] The chairperson sends a written follow-up to both the Scout and the Scoutmaster regarding the deficiencies and the course of action needed to correct them.[1]

## Appeal Procedures

The search results do not contain information about formal appeal procedures. However, they reference section 8.0.3.2 of the Guide to Advancement regarding "Initiating Eagle Scout Board of Review Under Disputed Circumstances,"[2] which suggests procedures exist but are not detailed in these sources.

## Additional Notes on Eagle Scout Applications

For Eagle rank specifically, a candidate must confirm all requirements have been completed before their 18th birthday, though the board of review may be conducted after the 18th birthday.[2] The council verifies the application and schedules the board of review only after verification is complete.[2] A board of review shall not occur until after the local council has verified the application.[2]
