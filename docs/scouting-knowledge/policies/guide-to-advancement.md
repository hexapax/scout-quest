### How Advancement Works (Section 4.2.1.0)
Advancement in Scouts BSA follows **four steps**: the Scout learns a skill, is tested on it, is reviewed by the unit leader via a Scoutmaster conference, and advances through a board of review. Once tested and signed off by an approved person, the requirement is met **and cannot be rescinded** (new clarification in Section 4.2.1.2).[1][4] Personal growth is the primary goal, using methods like the patrol system and Scout Oath/Law (Section 2.0.0.3).[1]

### Role of the Scoutmaster Conference (Section 4.2.1.3 and 8.0.3.0)
The Scoutmaster conference is a formal review with the unit leader (or delegate) to discuss progress, Scout spirit, and advancement plans. It occurs before each rank advancement and must be passed; Scouts **must** meet with their unit leader before starting merit badges (Section 7.0.0.3).[1][4] It focuses on encouragement, not re-testing skills.

### Board of Review Procedures and Rules (Section 8.0.4.0)
- **Who serves**: 3-6 members, primarily unit committee members; excludes the Scoutmaster, parents, or those involved in teaching requirements. For Eagle, district/council representatives may join.[1]
- **What can and cannot be asked**: Review Scout spirit, participation, and outlook; re-test skills only if mastery is in doubt. **Cannot** grill on merit badge details, quiz extensively, or deny for minor issues like uniform lapses—focus on growth, not punishment.[1]
- **How decisions are made**: Unanimous approval required; if denied, provide written reasons and reapplication timeline (at least one month wait). Boards certify rank achievement.[1][4]

### Partial Completion and Carryover Rules (Sections 4.2.3.0 and 9.0.1.8)
Partial merit badge work carries over to new counselors; no starting over unless the Scout chooses. Signed requirements are final and non-rescindable. Units track progress; denials (e.g., Eagle) must be documented by council within two weeks, with two-month appeal window for Scout/parents.[1][4]

### Time Requirements Between Ranks (Section 4.2.0.1)
Scouts BSA ranks have minimum time in position of responsibility (e.g., 4 months for Star/Life) and active participation periods, but no fixed calendar waits beyond age eligibility (e.g., First Class by age 13 not required).[1]

### Alternate Requirements for Disabilities (Section 10.0)
Fully reorganized: Provides **individualized accommodations** via unit leader assessment, registered medical statement, or Abilities Merit Badge counselor. Alternatives must align with requirements' purpose; no blanket waivers—focus on feasibility for special needs.[1][4]

### Appeal Procedures (Section 9.0.0.0)
Appeals start at unit committee chair, then district/council advancement committee, then National Advancement Team (topic 3.0.0.0). File within time limits (e.g., two months for Eagle denial); provide evidence. Councils cannot add/subtract from Guide rules.[1][5]
