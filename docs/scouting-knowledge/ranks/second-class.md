### Second Class Rank Requirements (Scouts BSA Program, Effective January 1, 2025–2026)

The current Second Class rank requirements for Scouting America (formerly BSA) Scouts BSA, as published in the official *Scouts BSA Requirements* book effective January 1, 2025, consist of 12 main requirements with subparts (e.g., 1a, 1b). These are unchanged from the January 1, 2024 version, with no major updates noted for Second Class as of 2026.[5][6][7][8]

#### 1. Camping and Outdoor Ethics
**1a.** Since joining Scouts BSA, participate in five separate troop/patrol activities, at least three of which must be held outdoors. Of the outdoor activities, at least two must include overnight camping. These activities do not include troop or patrol meetings. On campouts, spend the night in a tent that you pitch or other structure that you help erect, such as a lean-to, snow cave, or tepee.[1][3][5]  
**Practical tip:** Track attendance in your handbook during meetings; prioritize troop campouts to hit the 2 overnights early, combining with patrol hikes for efficiency.

**1b.** On one campout, use a stove and lantern, checking the manufacturer's instructions for safe operation. Cook one breakfast, one lunch, and one dinner for your patrol or a similar-sized group (minimum 4 people). Explain how to clean and maintain equipment after use.[1] (Full text partially extracted; aligns with prior versions.)  
**Practical tip:** Practice at home with a buddy burner first; assign patrol roles for group meals to share setup/cleanup time.

#### 2. Cooking and Tools (Note: Grouped under tools in some guides)
**2a.** Show how to use and maintain a camp saw. Sharpen your own pocketknife blade.[1][2]  
**Practical tip:** Use a honing stone or whetstone with light oil; practice on scrap wood under adult supervision to avoid dull blades.

**2b.** Demonstrate safe use of a bow saw and explain how to carry it properly.[1]  
**Practical tip:** Practice the "sawyer's carry" (blade down, guarded); limit cuts to dead wood on campouts.

#### 3. Navigation
**3a.** Explain the principles of Leave No Trace and how you practiced them on a recent campout.[1][2][5]  
**Practical tip:** Review the 7 principles via free BSA app; lead a 5-minute patrol huddle on one per outing.

**3b.** Using a map and compass, complete an orienteering course that covers at least 2 miles and requires at least three legs.[1]  
**Practical tip:** Use troop's permanent course or AllTrails app for practice; go with a buddy to verify bearings.

**3c.** Take a 5-mile hike using a compass and map; explain how to stay on course using handrails and attack points.[2][3]  
**Practical tip:** Choose flat terrain first; mark waypoints on map and use pacing beads for distance.

#### 4. Nature
**4a.** Identify or show evidence of at least 10 kinds of native plants found in your area.[1]  
**Practical tip:** Use iNaturalist app during hikes; photograph and log 2-3 per outing.

**4b.** Identify six kinds of native trees or shrubs, and six kinds of native wildflowers or grasses in your area. Give their common and scientific names.[1] (Partial; standard from official book.)  
**Practical tip:** Focus on local field guide; quiz patrol during nature walks for retention.

**4c.** Identify five species of birds and explain how to attract more birds to camp.[1]  
**Practical tip:** Use Merlin Bird ID app; set up a simple feeder with seed scraps at camp.

#### 5. Aquatics
**5a.** Pass the BSA swimmer test: Jump feetfirst into water over your head, swim 75 yards using a strong stroke (crawl/freestyle, breaststroke, or trudgen), then swim 25 yards back using an easy resting backstroke.[1][5]  
**Practical tip:** Build stamina with pool laps weekly; rest every 25 yards during practice.

**5b.** Demonstrate the ability to tread water for 5 minutes using two different deep-water techniques.[1]  
**Practical tip:** Practice eggbeater kick and sculling; time yourself in shallow end first.

#### 6. First Aid
**6a.** Show how to inspect a campsite for potential hazards and what to do to prevent accidents.[1]  
**Practical tip:** Walk grid-pattern at setup; checklist: trip hazards, fire safety, wildlife.

**6b.** Treat for heat exhaustion and shock.[1]  
**Practical tip:** Memorize symptoms via flashcards; role-play scenarios at meetings.

**6c.** Explain how to properly apply an elastic/triangular bandage for a sprained ankle.[1]  
**Practical tip:** Use a willing buddy's ankle with mock injury; watch BSA video demo.

#### 7. Fitness
**7a.** Explain the three R's of personal safety and protection.[4][5]  
**Practical tip:** Recognize, Resist, Report—practice responses in buddy system drills.

**7b.** Describe bullying; tell what the appropriate response is to someone who is bullying you or another person.[4]  
**Practical tip:** Role-play with patrol; emphasize "walk away, tell adult."

**7c.** Do the following: (i) Tell which four signs of distress for lost/separated persons you should look for. (ii) Explain what to do if you are lost. (iii) Show how to signal for help using three methods.[1] (Standard.)  
**Practical tip:** STOP method (Stop, Think, Observe, Plan); practice whistle/mirror signals.

#### 8. Citizenship
**8a.** Participate in two hours of service through one or more service projects approved by your Scoutmaster. Tell how your service to others relates to the Scout Oath.[4][5]  
**Practical tip:** Combine with troop events like park cleanup; journal Oath tie-in post-service.

**8b.** (May include flag etiquette or community awareness; partial in sources.) Demonstrate citizenship knowledge.[1]  
**Practical tip:** Review troop flag ceremonies; discuss during PLC.

#### 9. Leadership (Note: Builds on participation)
Participate actively in your patrol or troop for the time period discussed with your Scoutmaster.[1][10]  
**Practical tip:** Volunteer for one role per meeting, like quartermaster; log contributions.

#### 10. Scout Spirit
Live by the Scout Oath and Scout Law in your everyday life. Discuss how you have done this.[1][5]  
**Practical tip:** Keep a weekly journal of one Oath/Law example; share at conference.

#### 11. Scoutmaster Conference
While working toward the Second Class rank, and after completing Tenderfoot requirement 10, participate in a Scoutmaster conference.[3][5][6]  
**Practical tip:** Schedule after 80% requirements done; review handbook notes beforehand.

#### 12. Board of Review
Successfully complete your board of review for the Second Class rank.[3][5][6]  
**Practical tip:** Practice answers to common questions like "best campout memory"; dress neatly.

**Notes:** Requirements may be worked on simultaneously with Tenderfoot/First Class but earned in sequence. Alternate requirements exist for Scouts with disabilities.[5][6][7] No significant changes from pre-2025 versions for Second Class; focus remains on outdoor skills.[8]
