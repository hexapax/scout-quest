### Tenderfoot Rank Requirements (Scouts BSA Program, Effective January 2024–2026)

The following are the current **Tenderfoot rank requirements** from Scouting America (formerly BSA), as verified in official documents effective through 2026. These reflect the 2024 Scouts BSA Requirements book, with no major changes noted since 2024.[1][3][6] Scouts may work on Scout, Tenderfoot, Second Class, and First Class simultaneously but must earn them in sequence. Alternative requirements exist for Scouts with disabilities.[1][3][6]

#### **1. Camping and Outdoor Ethics**
**1a.** Present yourself to your leader, prepared for an overnight camping trip. Show the personal and camping gear you will use. Show the right way to pack and carry it.[1][2][6]  
*Practical tip:* Make a checklist of 10–12 essentials (e.g., sleeping bag, tent stakes, water bottle); pack in a backpack with heaviest items high and centered for balance—practice a 5-minute unpack/repaint demo.

**1b.** Spend at least one night on a patrol or troop campout. Sleep in a tent you have helped pitch.[1][2]  
*Practical tip:* Join your troop's next scheduled campout; arrive early to help pitch (stake corners first, then tension fly)—sign up via troop app or leader in advance.

**1c.** Tell how you practiced the **Outdoor Code** and **Leave No Trace** on campouts or outings.[1][2][6] (Note: Exact phrasing may vary slightly as "Explain how you demonstrated..." in some guides.)  
*Practical tip:* Keep a pocket notebook during outings; jot 2–3 examples per principle (e.g., "Packed out trash for LNT") and review with leader post-trip.

#### **2. First Aid**
**2a.** Show first aid for cuts and scratches, and for insect bites or stings.[1][4]  
*Practical tip:* Practice on a buddy with simulated wounds using gauze/bandages; memorize "clean, rinse, bandage" sequence—takes 2 minutes daily.

**2b.** Show first aid for a sprained ankle and for heat exhaustion.[1][4]  
*Practical tip:* Role-play with troop: elevate ankle on pack, apply RICE (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation); for heat, demo "cool, hydrate, rest"—use YouTube demos for visuals.

**2c.** Assemble a personal first-aid kit to carry on future campouts/hikes. Tell how each item is used.[1][4]  
*Practical tip:* Use a quart ziplock with 8 basics (bandages, tweezers, etc.); label and demo each item's use in under 60 seconds.

#### **3. Fitness**
**3a.** Record your best performance in push-ups, sit-ups (or curl-ups), and a standing long jump. (Do all three tests within 30 days and record results.)[1][4]  
*Practical tip:* Test weekly at troop meetings using a phone timer/app; aim for form over max reps—track in handbook for motivation.

#### **4. Cooking**
**4a.** On a campout, help in the kitchen, assist in preparing one meal, and clean up afterward.[1][2]  
*Practical tip:* Volunteer for patrol's easiest meal (e.g., foil dinners); follow "dirty, cook, clean" order—wear gloves, wash hands first.

#### **5. Citizenship**
**5a.** Demonstrate how to display, raise, lower, and fold the U.S. flag.[1][4]  
*Practical tip:* Practice at home with a 3x5 flag; follow flag code (hoist briskly, lower slowly)—video yourself for 2-minute review.

**5b.** Participate in a total of one hour of service through one or more service projects approved by your Scoutmaster. Explain how your service relates to the **Scout slogan** ("Do a Good Turn Daily") and **Scout motto** ("Be Prepared").[1][4]  
*Practical tip:* Join troop cleanup (e.g., park 1-hour event); log time/hours, link to motto (prepared to serve) in 1-paragraph journal.

#### **6. Knife Axe Saw**
**6a.** Show correct use of knife, ax, and saw. Demonstrate sharpening a knife.[1][2][4]  
*Practical tip:* Get troop "totin' chip" first; practice on soft wood (5 strokes per side on stone)—supervise always, store sheathed.

#### **7. Hiking**
**7a.** Explain the importance of the buddy system as it relates to personal safety on outings and at home. Use the buddy system on a troop/patrol outing.[1][4]  
*Practical tip:* Pair with same-size buddy; yell "Changing buddies!" when switching—practice on 1-mile hike.

**7b.** Hike ½ mile minimum (as a patrol or troop). Record starting/ending locations and date.[1][2]  
*Practical tip:* Use troop hike or neighborhood trail with GPS app; photo start/end signs for proof.

#### **8. Compass**
**8a.** Explain what to do if lost; demonstrate using a map and compass to find direction.[1][2]  
*Practical tip:* Memorize STOP (Stop, Think, Observe, Plan); practice orienteering at campout with troop compass.

#### **9. Ropework**
**9a.** Tie the **square knot**, **two half hitches**, and **taut-line hitch**. Explain purpose/use.[1][2][4]  
*Practical tip:* Practice 10x daily on paracord; teach a younger Scout using **Teaching EDGE** (Explain, Demonstrate, Guide, Enable) for dual credit.[4]

#### **10. Scout Spirit**
**10.** Demonstrate Scout spirit by living the **Scout Oath** and **Scout Law**. Tell how you have done your duty to God* and lived four points of the Scout Law in your everyday life.[1][3][4] (*Religious reference per Scout Oath.)  
*Practical tip:* Pick 4 Law points (e.g., Trustworthy, Kind); journal 1 daily example for a week, discuss in conference.

**11.** While working toward Tenderfoot (after Scout rank 7), participate in a Scoutmaster conference.[3][6]  
*Practical tip:* Schedule after 80% requirements; prepare by reviewing handbook notes—keep it 15–20 minutes.

**12.** Successfully complete your board of review for Tenderfoot.[3][6] (May be done after age 18; others before.)[4]  
*Practical tip:* Dress in Class A uniform; bring handbook—answer "What did you learn?" confidently.

### Recent Changes
- **2022:** Added 2c (personal first-aid kit).[4]
- **2024:** Kit requirement confirmed in official book (absent in 2023).[4]
- No further changes as of 2026; requirements stable since Jan 2024.[2][8] Prior versions (pre-2021) allowed older rules if started early, but post-2021 uses current.[1]
