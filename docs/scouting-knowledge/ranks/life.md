### Life Rank Requirements (Scouts BSA Program, Effective 2025-2026)

The Life rank requirements for Scouts BSA in Scouting America (formerly BSA) remain unchanged from the 2022 version as of 2025, with no major updates noted since August 2022 (minor prior changes affected lower ranks only).[1][3][4][6][7] Below is the full text of each requirement, numbered as per official sources, followed by a brief practical tip for efficient completion.

1. **Be active in your troop for at least six months as a Star Scout.**[1][2][3][4][7]  
   *Practical tip:* Track attendance via your troop's app or logbook from your Star board of review date; overlap this with other requirements to minimize extra time.

2. **As a Star Scout, demonstrate Scout spirit by living the Scout Oath and Scout Law. Tell how you have done your duty to God and how you have lived the Scout Oath and Scout Law in your everyday life.**[1][2][4][7]  
   *Practical tip:* Prepare a 5-minute talk with 2-3 specific examples (e.g., helping at church, being trustworthy with homework); practice with a peer for confidence.

3. **Earn **five more merit badges** (so that you have **11** in all), including **any three** of the following:**[1][2][4]  
   **a. Any of the following required for Eagle Scout:** First Aid, Lifesaving** (or Emergency Preparedness)**, Swimming** (or Hiking or Cycling)**, Environmental Science** (or Sustainability)**.[1][2][4]  
   **b. Tenderfoot - 4a and 4b (first aid)**[1][2][4]  
   **c. Second Class - 2b, 2c, and 2d (cooking/camping)**[1][2][4]  
   **d. Second Class - 3a and 3d (navigation)**[1][2][4]  
   **e. First Class - 4a and 4b (navigation)**[1][2][4][5]  
   **f. Second Class - 6a and 6b (first aid)**[1][2][4][5]  
   **g. First Class - 7a and 7b (first aid)**[1][2][4][5]  
   **h. Three requirements from one of the required Eagle merit badges, as approved by your Scoutmaster.**[1][2][4][5]  
   *Practical tip:* Choose Eagle-required badges first (e.g., First Aid overlaps with 3b/f/g); complete 3h early by picking one Eagle badge and getting Scoutmaster pre-approval for specific requirements.

4. **While a Star Scout, participate in **six hours of service** through one or more service projects approved by your Scoutmaster. At least **three hours** of this service must be **conservation-related**.**[1][4]  
   *Practical tip:* Join a troop conservation project (e.g., trail cleanup) for 3+ hours, then add a quick community service event; log hours immediately with photos.

5. **While a Star Scout, serve actively in your troop for **six months** in one or more of the following **positions of responsibility** (or carry out a Scoutmaster-approved leadership project):**  
   **Troop:** Patrol leader, assistant patrol leader, senior patrol leader, troop guide, Order of the Arrow troop representative, den chief, scribe, librarian, historian, quartermaster, junior assistant Scoutmaster, chaplain aide, instructor, webmaster, or outdoor ethics guide.  
   **Venturing crew:** President, vice president, secretary, treasurer, den chief, historian, guide, quartermaster, chaplain aide, or outdoor ethics guide.  
   **Sea Scout ship:** Boatswain, boatswain's mate, purser, yeoman, storekeeper, crew leader, media specialist, specialist, den chief, or chaplain aide.  
   **Lone Scout:** Leadership responsibility in your school, religious organization, club, or elsewhere in your community.[1][2][4]  
   *(Note: Assistant patrol leader is not approved for Life.)*[2]  
   *Practical tip:* Pick a low-meeting role like quartermaster or webmaster; overlap the six months with requirement 1 and document duties monthly.

6. **While a Star Scout, participate in a **Scoutmaster conference**.**[1]  
   *Practical tip:* Schedule right after completing 1-5; review your advancement folder beforehand to discuss progress confidently.

7. **Successfully complete your **board of review** for Life rank.**[1]  
   *Practical tip:* Practice answering "What is a good Scout?" with personal examples; boards can occur after age 18 if other requirements are met prior.[1]

**Key notes on changes:** No changes to Life requirements since August 2022 (prior 2022 updates were for Scout-Star ranks only).[4][6] Eagle-required merit badge flexibility (e.g., counting Hiking, Cycling, Swimming toward the "three") has been consistent since 2018.[1] Venturers/Sea Scouts follow the same requirements up to age 18.[1][3]
