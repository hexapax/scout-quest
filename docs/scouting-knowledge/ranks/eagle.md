The current Eagle Scout rank requirements for the Scouts BSA program, effective January 1, 2025, are detailed in the official Scouts BSA Requirements book.[1] These represent the most recent version as of 2026, with no major changes to the Eagle-specific requirements from prior versions (pre-2025), though merit badge updates effective January 1, 2026, may indirectly affect completion since Scouts starting badges after that date must use new versions.[1][3] Below is the **full text** of each requirement (sourced directly from the official document), including subparts, followed by a brief practical tip for efficient completion.

1. **Be active as a Life Scout for at least six months.**  
   *Practical tip:* Track your active participation dates via Scoutbook or troop records from your Life board of review; six months is calendar time, so start a leadership role immediately upon earning Life to overlap with other requirements.[1][4]

2. **Demonstrate Scout spirit by living the Scout Oath and Scout Law in your everyday life.**  
   *Practical tip:* Maintain a journal of daily examples (e.g., helping at home or school) to discuss confidently in your unit leader conference and board of review; tie it to troop discussions for reinforcement.[1]

3. **Earn a total of 21 merit badges, including the following:**  
   a. **First Aid**  
   b. **Citizenship in the Community**  
   c. **Citizenship in the Nation**  
   d. **Citizenship in Society** *(Note: This replaced Citizenship in the World effective 2022-2024; no change in 2025.)*  
   e. **Citizenship in the World**  
   f. **Communication**  
   g. **Cooking**  
   h. **Personal Fitness**  
   i. **Emergency Preparedness** *or* **Survival**  
   j. **Environmental Science** *or* **Sustainability*  
   k. **Personal Management**  
   l. **Swimming** *or* **Lifeguarding**  
   m. **Camping**  
   n. **Family Life** *(Note: Added as Eagle-required effective January 1, 2024; retained in 2025.)*  
   *Practical tip:* Prioritize Eagle-required badges early (aim for 13 by Star/Life); use troop merit badge workshops or counselors via Scoutbook to complete multiple in one session, tracking progress digitally to avoid duplicates.[1][7]

4. **While a Life Scout, serve at least six months in one or more positions of responsibility approved by your Scoutmaster or other designated leader.** *(Note: Clarified as "one or more" positions in recent updates for flexibility; six months total concurrent time required.)*  
   *Practical tip:* Choose a troop position like Patrol Leader or Quartermaster listed on the Eagle application; log hours weekly in a simple spreadsheet and get monthly Scoutmaster sign-off to ensure credit.[1][4][9]

5. **Plan, develop, and give leadership to others in a service project helpful to your religious institution, school, or community.** *(Details in Eagle Scout Service Project Workbook: Includes proposal approval, planning, execution, and final report.)*  
   *Practical tip:* Brainstorm 3-5 ideas early (e.g., park cleanup or school garden); submit proposal to council/district for quick approval (2-4 weeks typical), recruit 10+ volunteers via troop email, and photograph progress for your report.[1][4]

6. **Participate in a unit leader conference.**  
   *Practical tip:* Schedule after completing requirements 1-5; prepare by reviewing your advancement record and project—practice a 15-minute discussion on Scout spirit and leadership with a peer.[1][4]

7. **Successfully complete an Eagle Scout board of review.**  
   *Practical tip:* Request via your troop committee 30 days after #6; review the Scout Oath/Law, merit badges, and project impact—dress in full uniform and arrive with printed application and references.[1]

**Key notes on changes:** No structural changes to Eagle requirements in 2025 vs. 2024; however, Family Life became required in 2024, and 2026 merit badge tweaks (e.g., career exploration, safety) apply only to new starts.[1][3] Alternative requirements exist for disabilities via council advancement committee.[1] All must be completed before age 18 (extensions possible).[1][4]
