### Star Rank Requirements (Scouting America Scouts BSA Program, effective January 1, 2025)

The Star rank requirements, as outlined in the official Scouts BSA Requirements book effective January 1, 2025, emphasize leadership, service, and merit badge advancement for Scouts who have achieved First Class.[3][4][5] No major changes occurred for 2025-2026 from the January 2024 version; minor prior updates (e.g., August 2022 tweaks to positions of responsibility and Cyber Chip) remain in effect, with "assistant patrol leader" explicitly excluded as a valid position.[2][4][5] Below is the **full text** of each requirement, with practical tips.

1. **Be active in your troop for at least 4 months as a First Class Scout. (See page 24.)**[3][4][5]  
   *Practical tip:* Track attendance at 50%+ of troop meetings, outings, and service projects using your troop's app or logbook; start immediately after First Class board of review to minimize delay.

2. **As a First Class Scout, demonstrate Scout spirit by living the Scout Oath and Scout Law. Tell how you have done your duty to God and how you have lived the Scout Oath and Scout Law in your everyday life. (See pages 11–16 and 23.)**[1][3][4][5]  
   *Practical tip:* Prepare a 5-minute talk with 2-3 specific examples (e.g., helping a neighbor reflects "Helpful"); practice with your mentor before Scoutmaster conference.

3. **Earn six merit badges, including any four from the required list for Eagle. You may choose any of the 18 merit badges on the required list for Eagle to fulfill this requirement. See Eagle rank requirement 3 for this list. (See pages 28 and 422–425.)**

   (Eagle-required) _____ | _____  
   (Eagle-required) _____ | _____  
   _____ | _____  
   _____ | _____[3][4][5]  
   *Practical tip:* Prioritize Eagle-required badges like Camping or Citizenship in the Nation via summer camp; use troop merit badge workshops to complete two per session efficiently.

4. **While a First Class Scout, participate in six hours of service through one or more service projects approved by your Scoutmaster. At least three hours of this service must be conservation-related.**[1][3][4][5]  
   *Practical tip:* Combine with troop projects like park cleanups (counts as conservation); log hours on the service hours report form right after each event.

5. **While a First Class Scout, serve actively in your troop for four months in one or more of the following positions of responsibility (or carry out a Scoutmaster-approved leadership project to help the troop). (See pages 422-425.)**  
   **Scout troop.** Patrol leader, assistant senior patrol leader, senior patrol leader, troop guide, Order of the Arrow troop representative, den chief, scribe, librarian, historian, quartermaster, bugler, junior assistant Scoutmaster, chaplain aide, instructor, webmaster, or outdoor ethics guide.  
   **Venturing crew.** President, vice president, secretary, treasurer, den chief, historian, guide, quartermaster, chaplain aide, or outdoor ethics guide.  
   **Sea Scout ship.** Boatswain, boatswain’s mate, purser, yeoman, crew leader, media specialist, specialist, den chief, or chaplain aide.  
   **Lone Scout.** Leadership responsibility in your school, religious organization, club, or elsewhere in your community.[1][3][4][5]  
   *Practical tip:* Choose a low-drama role like quartermaster; serve consecutive months, get weekly feedback from Scoutmaster to confirm "active" status.

6. **Recertify in the First Class—Second Class—First Aid skills AND either (choose one):**  
   **a.** Complete the **Cyber Chip** requirements for grades 6-8 **OR** grades 9-12, as appropriate.  
   **b.** Complete the **Safety** merit badge.[1][4]  
   *Practical tip:* If you earned Cyber Chip for Scout rank in the same grade band, it's already done; otherwise, review 20-minute online modules and quiz—quickest option under 1 hour.

7. **While a Star Scout, participate in a Scoutmaster conference.**[4]  
   *Practical tip:* Schedule right after requirement 6; review your advancement log and prepare questions about Life rank to show readiness.

8. **Successfully complete your board of review for the Star rank.**[3][4][5]  
   *Practical tip:* Practice uniform inspection and requirement recall with a Troop Committee member; arrive 10 minutes early with handbook signed off.
